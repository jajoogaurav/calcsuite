What is SIP? – Systematic Investment Plan Explained

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) is the most popular way to invest in mutual funds in India. Instead of investing a large amount at once, SIP lets you invest a fixed amount every month — starting from as low as ₹500. This disciplined approach helps you build wealth over time through the power of compounding.

How SIP Returns Are Calculated

SIP returns are calculated using the future value formula with compounding:

FV = P × [(1 + r)n – 1] / r × (1 + r)

Where P is the monthly investment, r is the monthly return rate (annual rate ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the total number of months. For example, investing ₹10,000 per month for 10 years at 12% annual returns would grow to approximately ₹23.23 lakhs.

Why SIP is Ideal for Indian Investors

  • Rupee cost averaging — buy more units when markets are low, fewer when high
  • Power of compounding — returns earn returns over time
  • Disciplined investing — automate your wealth-building journey
  • Tax benefits — ELSS SIPs offer tax deductions under Section 80C
  • Flexibility — start, stop, increase, or decrease anytime

SIP vs Lump Sum Investment

While lump sum investing can yield higher returns in a consistently rising market, SIP reduces timing risk and is better suited for salaried individuals in India. Most financial advisors recommend SIP for long-term goals like retirement planning, children's education, and home down payment.

Frequently Asked Questions – SIP Calculator

What is SIP in mutual funds?
SIP (Systematic Investment Plan) is a method of investing a fixed amount regularly in mutual funds. Instead of a lump sum, you invest small amounts monthly. This helps in rupee cost averaging and reduces market volatility impact. Most Indian mutual fund AMCs like SBI, HDFC, and ICICI Prudential offer SIP options.
How are SIP returns calculated?
SIP returns use compound interest applied to each monthly installment separately. The formula FV = P × [(1+r)^n – 1] / r × (1+r) calculates the future value, where each monthly payment compounds independently from its investment date.
What is a good SIP return rate in India?
Equity mutual funds in India have historically delivered 12-15% annual returns over 10+ years. Large-cap funds typically give 10-12%, mid-cap 12-15%, and small-cap 14-18% (with higher risk). For conservative planning, use 12% for equity and 7% for debt funds.
Can I start SIP with ₹500 per month?
Yes! Many mutual funds in India allow SIP investments starting from ₹500 per month. Some funds even offer ₹100 SIP options. Popular choices include SBI Bluechip Fund, HDFC Top 100, and Axis Long Term Equity Fund (ELSS).
Is SIP better than Fixed Deposit?
Over long periods (10+ years), equity SIPs have historically outperformed FDs. While FDs offer guaranteed 6-7% returns, equity SIPs have averaged 12-15%. However, SIPs carry market risk and returns are not guaranteed. For a balanced approach, invest in both. Compare returns using our FD Calculator.
