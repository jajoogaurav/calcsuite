Frequently Asked Questions – EMI Calculator

What is car loan EMI?
Car loan EMI is the fixed monthly amount you pay to repay your car loan. It includes both principal and interest. For example, an ₹8 lakh car loan at 9% for 5 years has an EMI of about ₹16,607. Interest rates range from 7–12% in India depending on the bank, car model, and whether it's new or used.
How is home loan EMI calculated?
Home loan EMI uses the formula: EMI = P × r × (1+r)^n / ((1+r)^n – 1), where P is loan amount, r is monthly interest rate (annual ÷ 12 ÷ 100), and n is the total months. Home loans have rates from 8–10% with tenures up to 30 years in India. Our calculator shows the complete amortization with year-wise breakdown.
What is personal loan EMI and why is it higher?
Personal loan EMI is typically higher than home or car loan EMI because personal loans are unsecured (no collateral). Interest rates range from 10–24%, and tenures are shorter (1–5 years). A ₹5 lakh personal loan at 14% for 3 years has an EMI of ₹17,094. Use our calculator to check affordability before applying.
Which loan type has the lowest EMI?
Home loans generally have the lowest EMI because they offer the longest tenure (up to 30 years) and lowest rates (8–10%). For the same ₹10 lakh: Home loan EMI at 8.5%/20yr = ₹8,678; Car loan at 9%/5yr = ₹20,758; Personal loan at 14%/3yr = ₹34,178. Can I reduce my EMI by prepayment?
Yes! Making prepayments reduces your outstanding principal. You can either reduce your EMI or shorten tenure. For floating-rate home loans, RBI mandates zero prepayment penalty. Bike loan EMI is the monthly installment for two-wheeler loans. Rates range from 8–15% with tenures of 1–5 years. A ₹1.5L bike loan at 10%/3yr has an EMI of ₹4,840. Most NBFCs like Bajaj Finance and HDFC offer instant two-wheeler loan approval with minimal documentation.
